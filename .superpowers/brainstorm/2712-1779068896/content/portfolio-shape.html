How it becomes "real cinematic" (applies to whichever you pick)

The craft levers

  • Scroll = a camera, not a scrollbar — Lenis weighted easing; motion lags slightly behind input (your v16 0.12 lerp)
  • One light source, one palette — black + TMA blue energy, consistent grade across every section
  • Continuity of motion — every section hands a moving element to the next (the explosion's particles seed the manifesto)
  • Type as cinematography — beats reveal on scroll progress, not on load; few words, big, timed
  • Sound-design-ready pacing — deliberate holds & negative space between hits, never uniform
  • Grain + vignette + DOF — the film layer that kills the "website" feel (already in v16)

What kills cinematic (avoid)

  • 3D running the whole page (perf death + fatigue) — that's why we cap it at hero→1 section
  • Linear 1:1 scroll with no easing (feels like a spreadsheet)
  • Many fonts / many accent colors
  • Everything animating at once with no rest beats
  • AI-watermarked 720p source as final hero asset

My recommendation: A — The Release. It's the only one where the 3D pays off the story (rupture = "we release momentum") instead of being decoration, and it's the truest to a bold creative agency.
